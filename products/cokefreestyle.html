Design Diary

HOME META EVALUATION

Product 8: Coke Freestyle Machine

Introduction / Context

The Coca-Cola Freestyle machine is a touchscreen soda fountain found in dining halls and cafés. It allows users to choose from a wide variety of drinks and flavor combinations while also interacting with physical elements like placing a cup and dispensing a drink.

Affordances

Affordances are features that show users how something can be used. The touchscreen interface clearly suggests tapping through large buttons and drink options. The cup placement area also signals where to put a drink before dispensing.

Feedback

Feedback is how the system responds after a user takes an action. The machine provides visual and sometimes auditory feedback when a drink is selected and dispensed. However, small delays between selecting a drink and the machine responding can make users unsure if their input was registered.

Discoverability

Discoverability is how easy it is for users to find features in a system. While the main drink categories are easy to find, the large number of flavors and combinations can make it harder to quickly locate specific options, especially for new users.

Cognitive Load

Cognitive Load is the amount of mental effort needed to use a system. The wide variety of drink options increases the amount of thinking required to make a choice. Users may spend extra time deciding, which can slow down the overall interaction.

Hick’s Law

Hick’s Law states that the more choices a user has, the longer it takes to make a decision. Because the machine offers so many drink and flavor combinations, users may take longer to choose what they want, especially when they are unfamiliar with the interface.

Mapping

Mapping refers to the relationship between controls and their outcomes. Selecting a drink on the screen should directly connect to what is dispensed, but sometimes the connection between flavor selection and the final output is not immediately clear to users.

Constraints

Constraints are limits on how users can interact with a system. The machine requires a cup to be properly placed before dispensing, which prevents spills and incorrect use. This helps guide user behavior and avoid errors.

Final Recommendation

The Coca-Cola Freestyle machine provides a fun and customizable experience with strong affordances and interactive design. However, high cognitive load and too many choices can make the system less efficient for users. Improving organization and reducing decision complexity would make the experience faster and easier to use.
