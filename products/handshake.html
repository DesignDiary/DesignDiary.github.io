HOME META EVALUATION

Product 2: Handshake

Introduction / Context

Handshake is a job search and career platform that primarily focuses on connecting students or recent graduates with employers. It has many functionalities such as job browsing, job-application tracking, a slew of AI functions and training programs, and employer discovery all on the same platform.

Utility

Utility refers to how well a product provides the functions people actually need. Handshake has strong utility because it gives users access to job searching, profile building, application tracking, and employer discovery all in one platform. This can be very useful for students who need experience to get into the currently fairly competitive job market.

Usefulness

Usefulness is in regards to how well the product helps people complete the tasks they desire. Handshake is pretty useful because it supports students to find job openings. Handshake can also allow employers to manage different applications and listings which is also quite useful.

Chunking

Chunking refers to breaking large amounts of information into smaller pieces. Handshake uses chunking through job cards, employer profiles, and filter sections, which makes the site easier to process.

Hierarchy

Hierarchy describes how a design guides attention by showing what matters most first. Handshake does use hierarchy fairly well as job titles, locations, application actions, and other information stands out more immediately than less important information.

Hick’s Law

Hick’s Law explains that too many choices can slow decisions. Due to Handshake offering many job listings, it can cause user's choices to slow down and stagnate as they might overthink which job to apply to, or which opportunity to dig into deeper.

Customization

Customization means letting users adjust the experience to fit their needs. Handshake enables customization via profile updates and search preferences that change how users can interact with the platform and find jobs.

Final Recommendation

Overall, you should use Handshake if you're looking for jobs. Its largest issue is the overwhelming amount of information that it can present to users, but that being said, its chunked structure help manage that issue for students and employers alike.
