Product 6: Notepad

Introduction / Context

Windows Notepad is a barebones and stripped-down text editor that's been made for simple writing. Compared to most text editors, it has little functionality; however, this is because it prioritizes utility and efficiency over added bloat. For users who want to simply edit files or write small notes, Notepad has been a staple for decades.

Effectiveness

Effectiveness refers to how well a product performs at its given task. Notepad is super effective as it allows users to write notes with little to no friction. The app does not try to do much more than that, thus users are able to get their work done.

Efficiency

Efficiency refers to how fast a product is at completing its given task. Notepad's interface supports quick actions with few steps needed to reach them. This makes Notepad useful for quickly jotting ideas down, writing small code snippets, or temporarily storing data to copy elsewhere.

KISS

Keep it simple stupid refers to the idea that websites/products work best when kept simple and easy to use. Notepad follows KISS as simplicity is one of it's most apparent features. Notepad's interface largely depicts an open text box with a few options at the top, and this reduces confusion and keeps the user focused on the task.

Mental Models

A mental model refers to the heuristic in a person's head that they have when interacting with a product. Many people understand how text editors work if they have a computer, and Notepad essentially sets the baseline mental model for what a text editor should be. This makes it easy for the app to behave as intended given a user's prior experience editing text on computers.

Learnability

Learnability refers to the concept of how difficult it is to bring one's understanding of a product up enough to fully utilize said product. Notepad is very easy to learn due to having few functions to wrap one's head around. Thus, Notepad has a very low barrier to entry and a first-time user can generally wrap their head around how to type, save, and close a .txt file immediately.

Hierarchy

Hierarchy refers to a way to organize that shows what is most important first. The text are in Notepad is the most important aspect of the program's interface which creates a clear hierarchy. Additional controls remain small and at the top in simple drop-down menus, so that the user's attention remains focused on the content of the file they have opened.

Final Recommendation

Overall, you should use Notepad if you're on the Windows OS. Windows Notepad is a strong choice when you want speed and simpicity for basic text editing. It has a high factor of utility, good efficiency, and little to no learning entry which makes it a very usefu too for day-to-day tasks.
