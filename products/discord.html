Product 4: Discord

Introduction / Context

Discord is an application that allows gamers to stay connected to their friends while they play video games together. It has recently started to grow outside of its gamer base and promote itself as a hub for communities.

Hierarchy

A way to organize that shows what is most important first. Discord uses hierarchy in the way it organizes its servers which are all located on the left-hand side of the page and once you click into it then it shows server channels that are either text based or meet up hubs. On the home page it shows all your friends in a row after the servers and in the middle of the screen it shows who is online.

Customization

Customization allows the user to tailor their experience with the product to fit their preferences. There are a variety of ways to customize your experience on Discord such as custom profiles with different fonts and badges. In servers you can create a custom sound board to use when you are playing with your friends on the server. Unfortunately, some of the customizations mentioned are locked by Discord’s premium service.

Nudges

Nudges are ways that app try to guide a user to perform certain tasks. This can be related to push notifications which indicate a variety of things such as friends hopping into a server, unread messages, and friend recommendations. This continues to keep the user active on the site and encourages the user to foster their connections om the app.

Memorability

Memorability is how easy it is for a user to recall how to use a system after a certain time period away. Most of Discord is easy to remember how to use. The user, even after having some time away would easily be able to understand how to send a message, make a call, or join a server. Which are the basic functions of the app in general.

Paradox of Choice

The paradox of choice is when a user has too many choices and becomes overwhelmed. Discords can become cluttered if the user is involved in many channels or servers. This can be handled by the user creating folders, muting channels that they are not as active in, and leaving channels with the worst-case scenario.

Final Recommendation

Overall, I would recommend people to use Discord. It is easy to use, has seamless overview with most games, and an easy way to connect with friends or meet new people. The only problem would be that when it comes to running a server there are some functions that are difficult to understand.
